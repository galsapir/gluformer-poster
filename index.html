A Foundation Model for Continuous Glucose Monitoring Data

GluFormer learns generalizable representations of metabolic health from 10 million glucose measurements

Key Finding

A generative transformer trained on 10 million glucose measurements learns representations that predict diabetes onset and cardiovascular mortality 12 years in advance — outperforming gold-standard clinical markers.

Motivation

Continuous glucose monitors (CGMs) capture rich temporal dynamics of metabolic health, yet current clinical practice reduces these complex signals to simple summary statistics (e.g., average glucose, time-in-range).

Can we learn richer representations? Inspired by the success of large language models, we treat glucose time series as sequences and apply autoregressive self-supervised learning to capture latent metabolic patterns.

GluFormer Architecture

How It Works

  1. Tokenize — Continuous glucose values are discretized into tokens, analogous to words in natural language
  2. Pre-train — A 16-layer transformer learns to predict the next glucose token in an autoregressive fashion on >10M measurements
  3. Extract — The model's internal representations encode rich metabolic phenotypes
  4. Apply — These learned embeddings transfer to diverse downstream clinical prediction tasks

Key Takeaways

  1. Foundation model for CGM — Self-supervised learning on 10M+ glucose measurements captures rich metabolic representations
  2. Universal generalization — Validated across 19 cohorts, 5 countries, 8 devices, multiple disease states
  3. Clinical superiority — Outperforms HbA1c and standard CGM metrics for long-term risk prediction
  4. Actionable stratification — Identifies at-risk prediabetic individuals years before clinical deterioration
  5. Multi-modal capable — Integrates dietary data for personalized nutrition insights
